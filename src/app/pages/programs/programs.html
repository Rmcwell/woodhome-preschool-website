Programs Overview

At Wood Home Preschool, we offer developmentally appropriate programs for children from 18 months to 5 years old.

Toddler Program (18 months - 2.5 years)

Our toddler program focuses on creating a nurturing environment where your child can explore and develop their emerging independence. We emphasize:

  • Language development through stories, songs, and conversation
  • Sensory exploration and fine motor development
  • Social skills and emotional development
  • Beginning self-help skills
  • Gross motor development through indoor and outdoor play

Our low teacher-to-child ratio ensures that each toddler receives the attention and care they need during this important developmental stage.

Preschool Program (2.5 - 4 years)

Our preschool program builds on children's natural curiosity and desire to learn. Through play-based activities, we help children develop:

  • Pre-literacy and language skills
  • Basic mathematical concepts
  • Scientific thinking through hands-on exploration
  • Creative expression through art, music, and dramatic play
  • Social skills including cooperation, sharing, and problem-solving
  • Physical development through structured and unstructured play

Our preschool classrooms are arranged in learning centers that encourage children to make choices, follow their interests, and develop independence.

Pre-K Program (4-5 years)

Our Pre-K program is designed to prepare children for a successful transition to kindergarten. We focus on:

  • Early literacy skills including letter recognition, phonological awareness, and writing readiness
  • Mathematical concepts such as counting, patterns, sorting, and basic addition/subtraction
  • Scientific inquiry and critical thinking
  • Social studies and cultural awareness
  • Self-regulation and executive function skills
  • Advanced social skills and collaborative learning

Our Pre-K graduates enter kindergarten with confidence, curiosity, and the academic and social-emotional skills needed for success.
